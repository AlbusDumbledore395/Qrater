Indian Constitution

The Indian Constitution refers to the supreme legal document that governs the Republic of India. It serves as the framework for the organization, functioning, and powers of the government, as well as the fundamental rights and duties of citizens. The Indian Constitution was adopted by the Constituent Assembly on November 26, 1949, and came into effect on January 26, 1950, marking the establishment of the Republic of India.
